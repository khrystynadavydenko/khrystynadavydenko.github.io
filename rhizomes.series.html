RHIZOMES

Rhizome (from the ancient Greek "rhízōma" – "rootstock") is a nonlinear system of bonds, extending simultaneously in all directions and is infinite in its essence. One of the principles of such a system is the principle of vaccination, where each part of the rhizome can be combined with any other. The term comes from botany, where it denotes some types of plants’ rootstocks.
